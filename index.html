Hello World!

This is Tanvir. I am an undergraduate student at the University of Connecticut, majoring in Computer Science & Engineering and Electrical Engineering.

I like to code smart programs that can impact our lives in a positive way.

My undergraduate research work includes working under the supervision of Dr. Song Han and Dr. Shengli Zhou to research the use of software instead of the traditional hardware approach for implementing smart real-time wireless technologies.

I have also assisted Dr. John Ayers on his work to design and simulate different superlattice structures for metamorphic semiconductor devices.

I believe that intelligent software and the power of processing huge sets of data is the new frontier of technological advancement. From financial analysis, predicting customer preferences all the way to solving environmental issues - they all require us to analyze and learn from the data. As a reult, I am interested in exploring and working on human computer interaction, machine learning, artificial intelligence, big data, and software systems.

Currently I work at University of Connecticut Fine Arts Dean's Office where I do web-development and technical support. I have also worked as an Information Technology Intern at the Travelers Companies, and as a National Science Foundation Summer Undergraduate Researcher at the University of Connecticut.

Apart from my work and research, I try to do small projects in my spare time. From my interest in AI and ML, I worked on developing an AI opponent for Checkers, coding games such as Pong, Chess, explored projects using TensorFlow library, etc. One of the recent projects that I worked on was developing an Interactive Simulation of Cellular Automata.

When not coding, I play guitar, travel, or read books with a warm cup of coffee in my hand.

Feel free to contact me with any question or comment. Thank you!
